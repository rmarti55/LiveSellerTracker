# Evidence — Live Seller persona

Every claim in `persona.md` and `empathy-map.md` traces to something here. Two source types:
**(A)** the Whatnot 2026 State of Live Selling report + reseller press/community, and **(B)** a
primary-source teardown of a real seller, **@momsandcrumbs**, captured 2026-07. No invented stats.

---

## A. Market facts (Whatnot 2026 State of Live Selling + press)

- **Frequency = income.** Sellers going live 3–4×/week earn **40–70× more** than those who go
  1–2×/month; **daily** sellers earn **100–250× more**. Cadence is the single biggest growth lever.
- **Professionalizing fast.** **1 in 8 Whatnot sellers is now full-time** (+20% YoY). The number of
  sellers earning **$10K+/month more than doubled** YoY; **$1M+ lifetime sellers doubled** in 2025.
- **They're outgrowing the spare room.** ~**80%** of sellers now operate from commercial space
  (storefront/warehouse/office); **24%** rented additional space as they scaled.
- **The "one viral stream" is a myth.** The sellers who grow fastest "consistently show up, improve
  every stream, and learn what their audience wants" — not chasers of a single viral moment.
- **Top pains (press/community):** sourcing inventory (and not getting scammed on pallets), pricing,
  the **mental/emotional toll of slow shows** (taking it personally), unpredictable income, burnout,
  and reacting to algorithm/platform changes.
- **Getting started is a vetted funnel:** apply → get approved → first shows are small; focused
  single-category shows get placed better; phone + tripod + lighting is enough; ship within ~2 days.

## B. Anchor case study — @momsandcrumbs (real Whatnot seller)

A composite persona should be grounded in a real person's arc. Moms & Crumbs (founder "Cindy") shows
the full **aspiring → growth → scaled** journey and names the pains in her own words.

**Store stats (captured 2026-07):** 4.9★ · **67,302 reviews** · **344.2K sold** · 142.5K followers ·
1.1K following · 1-day avg ship · **22 upcoming shows scheduled**. Sells women's fashion, shoes,
accessories, beauty, and liquidation. Runs **daily, themed, recurring** shows ("Lululemon MONDAY",
"BOUTIQUE Tuesday", "$1 Starts", "Pallet Pulls", community-host shows) and a **2nd account**
(@momsandcrumbsdeals). This is a scaled, professionalized operation.

**Founder origin story (from her paid guide copy) — the emotional core:**
- *"Before the warehouse… Before truckloads… Before weekly pallets… We were **$60,000 in debt**."*
- *"I made mistakes. I lost money. I learned the hard way."*

**Deepest pain = sourcing risk (her words):**
- *"Wholesale can grow your business FAST… or create a **dead pile in your garage**."*
- *"**Most resellers scale too early** — they buy pallets before: building an audience, **testing
  sell-through speed**, understanding grading, calculating freight, preparing storage."*
- The knowledge gaps she sells against: when it's safe to buy wholesale, the "20–100 item rule"
  before scaling, evaluating suppliers without getting scammed, Grade A/B/C, shelf-pull vs overstock
  vs customer returns, freight + lift-gate costs, residential vs commercial delivery, estimating
  **real** profit margins, what to ask before sending money.

**What she productized (reveals what sellers will pay for):** a free beginner checklist (lead
magnet), a **$27** wholesale-sourcing guide + video, a **$47** "30-Day LiveSeller System", a
**$97/mo** community, **$997–$1,497** 1:1 coaching, a podcast, and an email list. Bonuses in the $27
guide: an **inventory-tracking sheet**, a **pallet-evaluation checklist**, supplier-protection tips,
and a **trusted wholesale source list**.

**Aspiring-seller voices (her community testimonials) — the early-stage emotional state:**
- *"I just finished my first show, thanks God first and thank you girls for the support… **I sold 8
  items** 🥰."*
- *"our fifth show… attendance numbers just keep getting better… we sold our very first item… **I
  got a little emotional** when we made that sale, but it was a huge milestone."*
- *"Thank you so much, Cindy, for your training and support!! **I needed this!!**"*

**Reading of the evidence:** sellers don't just want product to sell — they want to **de-risk
decisions** (what to source, will it sell fast, what to charge, when to go live, how the winners
operate) and they want **proof of progress** against an emotionally punishing, unpredictable grind.
That is exactly what a market-intelligence product provides.
