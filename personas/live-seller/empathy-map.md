# Empathy Map: The Live Seller (Bri)

> Companion to [`persona.md`](./persona.md); grounded in [`evidence.md`](./evidence.md). The classic
> four quadrants (Says / Thinks / Does / Feels) plus Pains and Gains. Use it to sanity-check that a
> feature meets the seller where she actually is emotionally, not just functionally.

---

## 🗣️ SAYS (out loud — shows, community, socials)
- "$1 to start! Who wants it?! Let's gooo 🎉"
- "I just finished my show — I sold 8 items 🥰 thank you all for the support!"
- "Where is everyone sourcing from? I don't want to get scammed on a pallet."
- "What night is best to go live? I feel like nobody's on."
- "How is she getting 2,000 viewers and I'm getting 40?"

## 🧠 THINKS (private, often unspoken)
- "Can I actually make this my full-time income and quit my job?"
- "If I buy this lot and it doesn't sell, that's money I can't afford to lose."
- "Am I pricing too low? Am I leaving money on the table?"
- "Is it me, or was it just a slow night? Should I have gone live at all?"
- "The big sellers know something I don't."

## ✋ DOES (behaviors)
- Goes live 3–4 nights/week after the kids are down; runs $1 starts, bundles, giveaways.
- Sources by gut from thrift runs, FB groups, and suppliers — second-guessing every buy.
- Photographs, lists, packs, and ships within the 2-day window — solo.
- Watches bigger sellers' shows and reseller YouTube/TikTok for tips; leans on community/Discord.
- Tracks inventory in a notebook or loose spreadsheet; counts each night's take manually.

## ❤️ FEELS
- **Hopeful & ambitious** — believes the $10K/mo dream is reachable.
- **Anxious** — about wasted inventory spend, unpredictable income, and getting scammed.
- **Exposed on slow nights** — a quiet show feels personal ("is it me?").
- **Proud & emotional on wins** — "I got a little emotional when we made that sale."
- **Stretched thin** — solo operator juggling sourcing, hosting, shipping, family; burnout looms.

---

## 🩹 PAINS (what's blocking her)
- **Sourcing risk:** no way to know if a lot will sell → fear of the "dead pile in the garage."
- **Pricing blind:** no reliable read on what items actually fetch live.
- **Timing guesswork:** unsure when/how often to go live for the best turnout.
- **No competitive visibility:** can see *that* big sellers win, not *how*.
- **Emotional volatility:** slow shows + unpredictable income with no objective progress signal.
- **Tool overwhelm:** no time or appetite for anything complex or spreadsheet-y.

## 🎁 GAINS (what success looks like)
- **Confident sourcing:** buys what's proven to sell fast → less dead stock, protected cash.
- **Right pricing:** starts/prices items where the market actually is.
- **Optimized cadence:** goes live at high-traffic times, at a sustainable frequency.
- **Competitive edge:** copies what the winners do (categories, prices, timing).
- **Proof of progress:** objective growth trend that steadies her through slow nights.
- **Calm + time back:** one-glance answers, a clear "do this next," so she can focus on selling.

---

## Design implications (how features should *feel*, not just function)
- **Reduce fear, not just inform.** Frame sourcing/price data as "safe to buy / risky" and "this sells
  fast / slow," not raw tables.
- **Reassure on the downside.** Progress-over-time views should contextualize a slow night as normal
  variance, not failure.
- **One glance, plain language.** She's on her phone, mid-grind, non-technical. Every screen answers
  one question and suggests one action.
- **Make her look like a pro.** Competitor teardowns and price confidence let her punch above her stage.
